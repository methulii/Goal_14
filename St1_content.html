Life Below Water

Supporting Global Goal 14 to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources.

REDUCE MARINE POLLUTION

By 2025, the goal is to prevent and substantially reduce marine pollution, focusing on land-based activities such as marine debris and nutrient pollution.

PROTECT AND RESTORE ECOSYSTEM

By 2020, it's essential to sustainably manage and protect marine and coastal ecosystems, enhancing their resilience and restoring them to ensure healthy and productive oceans.

REDUCE OCEAN ACIDIFICATION

To mitigate ocean acidification, enhanced scientific cooperation at all levels is crucial for minimizing its impacts effectively.

INCREASE SCIENTIFIC KNOWLEDGE, RESEARCH AND TECHNOLOGY FOR OCEAN HEALTH

To improve ocean health and support developing countries, enhancing scientific knowledge, research capacity, and the transfer of marine technology is essential. This effort, guided by the Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission's criteria, aims to boost the sustainable use of marine biodiversity, particularly benefiting small island developing States and least developed countries.

IMPLEMENT AND ENFORCE INTERNATIONAL SEA LAW

Enhancing ocean conservation and sustainable resource use involves implementing international law, such as the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea. This framework guides efforts to protect and manage oceans for long-term sustainability, as emphasized in global commitments like "The future we want" (paragraph 158).

CONSERVE COASTAL AND MARINE AREAS

By 2020, the goal is to conserve at least 10% of coastal and marine areas, following national and international laws and using the best scientific data available. This effort aims to protect these vital ecosystems and ensure their long-term health and productivity.
